Who this is for
The gap between having resources and knowing how to use them
Most college prep resources were built for students with counselors, class ranks, and four years of strategic preparation. If you're homeschooled, you've probably sat in a workshop that didn't quite apply to you — spending more time translating the advice than absorbing it.
For many homeschool students, college prep doesn't begin until senior year. The earlier years are just coursework — no one connecting the dots between what you're doing and where you're going. Bright Start Initiative was built specifically for students like you, by someone who was one.

About Ashley
Ashley is a homeschool graduate and first-year college student at Lamar University, where she conducts research on social capital differences between homeschool and elite preparatory school students. She founded Bright Start Initiative because she knows firsthand what it feels like to navigate college without a roadmap — and believes no student should have to figure it out alone.
