About Bright Start Initiative
The support system we wish we had
Too many homeschooled students enter the college journey without the guidance they deserve — not because they aren't capable, but because the resources simply weren't built with them in mind. We know that feeling firsthand. The tools that did exist often felt forced and discouraging, rather than empowering.
That's why Bright Start Initiative exists: to be the support system we wish we had. Through group workshops and a whole lot of encouragement, we walk alongside homeschooled students as they build their transcripts, navigate applications, and pursue scholarships. Most importantly, we create space for students to dream big and own their unique story. We are here to elevate the overlooked and underrepresented — because your path to college may look different, but your potential is anything but.

Frequently asked questions
Things we get asked a lot
Is Bright Start Initiative really free?
Group workshops and 1-on-1 consultations are completely free — no hidden fees, subscriptions, or upsells. Personalized 1-on-1 sessions (essay coaching, transcript help, etc.) are available on a sliding scale based on your situation.
Do I have to be fully homeschooled to attend?
Not at all. BSI is built for homeschoolers, but any non-traditional student who feels like mainstream college prep resources weren't made for them is welcome here.
What grade should I be in to start attending workshops?
We recommend starting as early as 9th or 10th grade. The earlier you understand how college prep works, the more strategic you can be. But it's never too late — 12th graders are absolutely welcome.
How do the workshops work?
All sessions are held live on Zoom. You register through our booking link, receive a confirmation with the Zoom details, and show up at the scheduled time. Sessions are small, interactive, and focused on practical guidance.
What if I can't make the scheduled time?
Most workshop topics repeat on a rotating basis, so you'll have another chance to attend. You can also book a free 1-on-1 consultation to get personalized guidance on any topic.
Can parents attend?
Absolutely. Many of our topics — especially FAFSA, transcripts, and documentation — are just as useful for parents navigating the process alongside their student.
What personalized services do you offer?
Ashley offers personalized 1-on-1 support in four areas: extracurricular building, college essay writing, transcript building, and scholarship support. All on a sliding scale — pricing is based on your situation. Email [email protected] to get started.
What does sliding scale pricing mean?
It means the price is based on what you're able to contribute, not a fixed rate. Nobody is turned away because of cost. If you're not sure what you can afford, just say so when you reach out and Ashley will work with you.

Personalized services
Deeper support when you need it

For students who need focused, individual guidance beyond what workshops offer. All services are sliding scale — pricing is based on your situation, not a fixed rate. Nobody is turned away because of cost.

EC Building ▼

Figuring out extracurriculars as a homeschooler is genuinely harder than it sounds. Ashley works with you to audit what you're already doing, identify gaps, build a sustainable EC plan, and coach you on how to present your activities on the Common App. Best for students in 9th–11th grade building intentionally, or 12th graders who need to frame what they already have.

College Essay Writing ▼

Your story is different — and different is interesting. Ashley works with you to find your strongest essay angle, develop a narrative arc that feels natural, and revise your personal statement and supplements through multiple drafts. Honest, specific feedback. Not just encouragement.

Transcript Building ▼

A homeschool transcript done well tells a clear, credible story of your academic journey. Ashley helps you build a course list with accurate titles and grades, write course descriptions that demonstrate rigor, format it professionally, and handle gaps or unconventional subjects. Best for families in 10th–12th grade starting to think seriously about documentation.

Scholarship Support ▼

Most scholarship databases weren't built with homeschoolers in mind. Ashley helps you find the ones that were, build a scholarship calendar with deadlines, strengthen your scholarship essays, and navigate FAFSA and financial aid appeals. Best for 11th and 12th graders preparing to apply alongside college applications.

Meet our founder
Ashley
Hi! My name is Ashley, and I'm the founder of Bright Start Initiative. I attended Texas Online Preparatory School and graduated in 2025. Although my time at TOPS helped me grow as a person and learn a lot about myself, I found that I was missing important tools for my future college plans. I faced several rejections from my dream schools — and it was devastating.
When I started my first semester at Lamar University, I decided to turn those experiences into fuel. I built BSI because no homeschool student should be overlooked in the admissions process simply due to a lack of resources. I'm now in my second year of college, conducting research in my school's psychology department — and I'm proof that your start doesn't define your story.
